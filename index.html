Managing Liwa's media coverage is one of the most logistically complex operations in UAE festival production. We've built the infrastructure to handle it seamlessly — for three years running.

23 consecutive days · Daily content delivery

The Daily Workflow

From the moment the festival opens to the moment it closes, our team is on the ground — capturing, editing, reviewing, and publishing. Not the next day. Not the next week. The same day. Our delivery model is built on two commitments: real-time coverage as moments happen, and same-day delivery of edited content — every day of the festival.

1

Morning Briefing & Schedule Review

Coordinate with event directors and DCT leads on the day's activities, key moments to capture, and any last-minute changes across all festival zones.

2

Full-Day Multi-Zone Coverage

Filmmakers, drone operators, and photographers deployed across the Village simultaneously — the Souk, family zones, dining, heritage areas, and live performances.

3

On-Site Editing & Same-Day Delivery

Editors work in real-time as footage comes in. Reels and stories are cut, colour-graded, and ready for review before the day ends — not tomorrow.

4

Feedback, Revisions & Publishing

Our office team handles the feedback loop, coordinates rounds of edits, and ensures everything goes live with flawless timing and captions — stories, reels, posts, all platforms.

Technical Approach

Our production model is built around continuous presence, parallel workflows, and zone-based flexibility — allowing us to capture everything simultaneously and deliver without delay.

🗺️

Multi-Zone Deployment

Camera teams are distributed across the Village daily — the Souk, Shams Liwa, Meydan, Wanasa, Adrenaline & Tahadi zones, dining, heritage areas, and performances. A standby team remains available for urgent requests, VIP moments, and last-minute coverage needs.

⚙️

Parallel Capture & Edit

Selected files are transferred to editors while camera teams continue shooting. Editing begins mid-day, not at the end of it — enabling same-day turnaround across all content formats without a bottleneck.

📋

Daily Briefing & Zone Assignment

Every production day begins with a schedule review by the Director and Project Manager. Team members are briefed, zones assigned, and priorities confirmed in line with live event requirements and client direction.

🗄️

Organized Asset Delivery

All captured content is catalogued, backed up, and delivered in an organized structure — ready for client use, stakeholder access, archiving, and future reference.

06 · Learnings
What We've Learned

Two Years of Insights.
Built Into Year Three.

We don't just deliver — we analyse, reflect, and improve. Every season makes us sharper. Here's what our data and experience have taught us.

Content Strategy
🚁

Drone Shots & Kids Content = Viral Fuel

Our highest-performing videos consistently featured aerial drone footage and content involving children. Single reels hit 2.6M and 1.8M views respectively — a formula we'll scale in 2026-27.

Audience Growth
📅

Posting Frequency Drives Algorithmic Growth

5 posts per day and an average of 12 reels per week produced a 20.7% page growth in just 4 weeks, with 57.94% of engaged users being non-followers — proving the reach potential is enormous.

Engagement
🎤

Audience Testimonials Drive Shares

Visitor feedback videos consistently outperformed polished announcements — human voices and real stories resonate and drive shares far more than branded posts.

Demographics
🌍

UAE-Heavy Audience — Expansion Opportunity

~63% of followers are UAE-based. We know the next frontier: the wider GCC and MENA region, where cultural affinity for Liwa's heritage content is high and the audience is largely untapped.

Audience Fit
👨‍👩‍👧

Family Content Is the Village's Superpower

The Village's audience is family-first — parents, kids, and community groups. Content built around genuine family moments consistently outperformed here, which is exactly why a dedicated, family-focused account works.

Content Strategy
🎯

Distinct Audiences Need Distinct Content

Our seasons taught us that the Village audience responds most to warm, authentic, family-and-heritage content — a distinct voice from the Festival's high-energy feed. That insight shapes how we plan @LiwaVillage's content for 2026–27.

Our read of the landscape, based on public profiles and each event's reputation in the category — and what it suggests for Liwa's 2026–27 direction.

Global Village and Sheikh Zayed Festival appear to lean heavily on consistent family and lifestyle content — the kind that builds a broad, returning audience. That is exactly the audience @LiwaVillage is built to own — a dedicated home for family and heritage content.

Global Village and Sheikh Zayed Festival set the bar for family and cultural storytelling that builds a loyal, returning audience. It's a useful reference for the warmth and consistency we aim for on @LiwaVillage — authentic, family-first, and built for repeat visits.

The regional giants grow through reach beyond their borders

AlUla Moments is known for scaling reach through celebrity and influencer activations aimed at an international audience. It suggests a model @LiwaVillage could draw on to grow beyond a purely local base — reaching GCC families and culture-seeking travellers.

Authentic heritage content is underused across the category

Based on public profiles, most of the field leans on polished promotion over authentic, human heritage storytelling. With an Emirati native voice and genuine access to the Village's craft, food, and community, this is a space @LiwaVillage can own.

Own the Family Audience

The Village is Liwa's family and community hub — but that story needs a dedicated voice to land. A focused account lets us speak directly to families, parents, and children rather than competing with the Festival's high-energy feed.

Measured by: family-segment engagement and growth in returning followers.

Build Community, Not Just Broadcast

Coverage has leaned on promotion over participation. The audience's own voice is the most underused asset we have — and the most powerful driver of genuine engagement.

→ A structured UGC amplification strategy and community takeovers
→ Short, authentic interviews with real visitors and the local community
→ Contests and interactive activations that reward participation
Measured by: UGC volume, comment and DM response, and engagement rate.

Deepen Content & Formats

Highlight reels alone don't build a year-round following. Varied formats keep the audience engaged between editions — sustaining awareness long after the gates close.

→ A broader format mix: visitor testimonials, community interviews, heritage & immersive content

🗣️ Visitor Testimonials

Real Voices of Liwa

Short, on-the-spot testimonials captured with real visitors — families, first-timers, returning guests, and the vendors and artisans who make the Village. Authentic, unscripted reactions that build credibility and show the Village through the eyes of those who live it.

🎤 Interview Series

Voices of Liwa

Short, on-the-ground interviews with visitors, families, local residents, and the Village's artisans and vendors. Real reactions and authentic stories — the relatable, highly shareable content that builds community and surfaces the audience's own voice.

🏛️ Heritage Series

The Story of Liwa

Short documentary-style reels exploring Emirati heritage, craft, and the cultural depth of the Village — the traditions and stories that give it its soul.

👨‍👩‍👧 Family Content

The Village Through Their Eyes

Content told from a family's perspective — kids on the rides, families exploring the Souk, parents making memories. The everyday magic of the Village, seen by the people who live it.

📣 UGC Amplification

Community Takeovers

A structured strategy to surface and amplify user-generated content from visitors — the most underutilised asset in the previous seasons. Real people, real reactions, real credibility.

🎧 Sensory Series

The Sounds of the Village

Immersive, ASMR-style short-form: the buzz of a busy Souk, sizzling food stalls, children's laughter, traditional music, and the hush of the desert at dusk. Distinctive, deeply on-brand sensory content that stops the scroll and feels unmistakably Liwa Village.
